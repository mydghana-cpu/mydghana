About MYD

We believe the collective energy of young people is Ghana's greatest untapped resource. The Movement for Youth Development was born to channel that energy.

Who We Are

Ghana's Youth Are Not a Problem to Be Solved —
They're the Solution.

The Movement for Youth Development (MYD) was founded in July 2019 by a group of young changemakers dedicated to channeling the collective energy of Ghana's youth into self-development and impactful community service.

We operate as a non-profit organization — dba Betterway Group — focused on three critical mandates: Capacity Development, Governance & Advocacy, and Community Resilience.

Since our inception, we have reached over 35,000 young people, created a network of 150+ volunteers, and built programs designed to impact millions across Northern Ghana and the nation.

Our Vision

Equal Access.
Equal Opportunity.

We envision a Ghana where all young people have equal access to economic opportunities, quality education, and are drivers of positive change in their communities.

Our Mission

Secure Jobs.
Promote Education.
Drive Change.

Our mission is to secure and create decent jobs, promote education, advocate for good governance, and influence a positive mindset for young people in Ghana.

Legal Identity

Registered & Accountable

MYD operates as a non-profit organization registered in Ghana under the name Betterway Group (dba — doing business as — Movement for Youth Development). We are headquartered at the Old State Construction Building, Tamale, Northern Region, Ghana.

01
Capacity Development
We invest in the skills, knowledge, and practical competencies of young Ghanaians — through agricultural training, digital literacy, financial education, and leadership development — to ensure they are equipped to participate in and drive economic growth.
02
Governance & Advocacy
We create structured platforms for youth to engage authentically with local and national decision-makers. From our Meet-the-Mayor conference to formal Youth Manifesto petitions, we ensure young voices translate into policy action.
03
Community Resilience
We train youth to become agents of peace and community stability. Through the YPS program, we build social cohesion, support conflict prevention, and position young people as active architects of community security.

Our Journey

Building a
Movement

From a small group of changemakers in Tamale to a growing organisation with national ambitions — here's how we got here.

July 2019
MYD Founded
A group of young changemakers in Tamale establish the Movement for Youth Development, operating as Betterway Group, to channel youth energy into self-development and community service.
2020 – 2021
First Programs Launched
The MYD Volunteer Program and Meet-the-Mayor Youth Conference take shape. Youth delegates engage District Assembly leadership for the first time in structured policy dialogue.
2022 – 2023
Youth Farm Project Developed
MYD designs and pilots the Youth Farm Project — securing farmland agreements with local chiefs, launching Climate-Smart Agriculture training, and integrating farm management technology.
October 2024
#YouthForPeace Townhall
Youth Action Plan on Peace & Security Townhall held in Tamale ahead of Ghana's general elections. #YouthForPeace campaign reaches over 1 million people via 2 radio stations and 1 TV station.
2025 – Present
Scaling Across Northern Ghana
35,000+ youth reached. 5,000+ direct beneficiaries. 150+ volunteers. Youth Farm Project operating across all 5 Northern regions with a 100-youth pilot cohort underway.
